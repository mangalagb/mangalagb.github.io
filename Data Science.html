Toronto Subway System Congestion Analysis

Gowri, Tue 29 December 2020, Data science, data visualization

Data Science, Data Visualization

Being a daily commuter in Toronto's subway system(TTC), I found myself stuck many times in the train. The announcement by the automated voice warning of congestions still rings clearly through my head!

It got me wondering if I could put numbers to this frustration of mine and show my friends of what pleces to avoid during their commute.

So I collected data about the metro system from the City of Toronto's open data catalog(https://www.toronto.ca/city-government/data-research-maps/open-data/)

This is the metadata of the dataset.

This information is available in a monthly format. I then proceeded to aggregate and group the data for a single year.

Then to plot the delays on the city map, I have used OpenStreetMaps (https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=4/48.14/-103.72) and a wonderful mapping library in Python called Folium (https://python-visualization.github.io/folium/).

In [44]:
import folium
import json

with open('./data/subway_delays_map_data.txt', "r") as read_file:
        subway_file = json.load(read_file)
map = folium.Map(location=[43.6706216, -79.3865296], zoom_start=11)

for row in subway_file:
    name = row[0]
    congestion = row[1]
    coordinates = row[2]
    latitude = coordinates['lat']
    longitude = coordinates['lng']
    
    folium.Circle(
      location=[latitude, longitude],
      popup=name,
      radius=congestion/3,
      color='crimson',
      fill=True,
      fill_color='crimson',
        fill_opacity=1,
   ).add_to(map)
